Graph Databases and Real-World Applications

November 27, 2025

Reflecting on my experience with ArangoDB during my internship at Zenteiq. Graph databases offer unique advantages for modeling complex relationships, and I'm excited to explore more use cases.

Traditional relational databases struggle with highly connected data - this is where graph databases shine. During my internship, I worked on designing schemas that could efficiently handle course recommendation systems based on user behavior and course relationships.

ArangoDB's multi-model approach allowed us to combine document storage with graph traversal capabilities. Writing AQL queries to find patterns in user click-through behavior was fascinating - it revealed insights that would have been nearly impossible to discover with SQL joins.

The performance improvements we achieved (40% better data analysis fidelity) came from optimizing graph traversal algorithms and strategic indexing. I learned that graph databases aren't just about storing connections - they're about making those connections queryable and actionable.

Moving forward, I'm keen to explore how graph neural networks can be combined with graph databases for even more powerful analytics.

Winter Arc: Leveling Up My Skills

November 26, 2025

Starting my winter arc with a focus on deepening my technical expertise. Planning to explore deep learning frameworks, advanced statistics, and preparing for potential doctoral research.

The winter arc is about intentional growth during these months. As a corporate professional, I'm dedicating time outside work to build skills that will prepare me for doctoral studies. The goal isn't just learning - it's about building a strong foundation for research.

My focus areas include: Deep Learning (PyTorch and TensorFlow), Advanced Statistical Methods, Research Methodology, Academic Writing, and exploring potential research areas in ML/AI that interest me.

I'm approaching this systematically - reading papers daily, implementing algorithms from scratch to truly understand them, and maintaining a research journal to document insights and questions. The idea is to transition from being a practitioner to a researcher who can contribute original ideas to the field.

While I'm not sure exactly when I'll pursue my PhD, I'm using this time to ensure that when the opportunity comes, I'll be ready. It's about building momentum, developing research discipline, and discovering what truly excites me in this vast field.
